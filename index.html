About Me

My name is Molly Culot, I am sixteen years old and a junior at Tappan Zee High School, located in Rockland County. I started programming my freshman year by taking the entry level classes offered at my school. In my sophomore year, I joined every computer science related extracurricular my school had to offer. This year, I have taken on many leadership roles in these activities, which has helped me become a leader within my community. I am continuing to help grow the computer science program in my school by encouraging other students to learn about programming, and promoting STEM to younger grades by showing them projects I have worked on. My favorite project that I am currently working on is a program that helps students located in Sub-Saharan Africa access STEM related education. It’s so rewarding to share my love of programming with those who wouldn’t otherwise have access to it. When I'm not coding, I enjoy playing the guitar, tennis, skiing, and working my part-time job at Starbucks.

I presented the robot at this event, and also created a game the children played called Carl, a platformer game where students could make him jump, move, and other simple mechanics meant to showcase programming. I was the announcer at this event and made sure all kids went to every station they were supposed to be at.

Robotics
Kiwimbi Collaboration
In October 2022, I attended a meeting with members of Kiwimbi Global, an organization dedicated to providing education to students located in Sub-Saharan Africa. In this meeting, the one thing I noticed about their program was it was all based around reading and writing and they had limited use of technology throughout their curriculum. I began to connect the robotics team with their organization, and wanted to somehow incorporate STEM into their lessons. My team donated Scratch books to them, allowing them to begin to create their own block programs. I created videos for them and organized them onto a website, teaching them how to create google and Scratch accounts. I also had members of my team create videos to walk students through certain problems in the books. Once they finish those, they will start programming VEX robots. My ultimate goal is for their school to eventually create a FIRST robotics team with assistance from my team. I meet with them on a video call every other Saturday to answer any questions they may have, and help them however is necessary.

Internships
During my sophomore year, I took on my first internship as a web developer for the Turtles of Western Highway and assisted them in any digital issues they came across. The Turtles of Western Highway is an organization dedicated to helping turtles cross Western Highway, a local street in Rockland County, where turtles cross in order to lay their eggs.
