About Me

My name is Molly Culot, I am seventeen years old and a senior at Tappan Zee High School, located in Rockland County. I started programming my freshman year by taking the entry level classes offered at my school. In my sophomore year, I joined every computer science related extracurricular my school had to offer. This year, I have taken on many leadership roles in these activities, which has helped me become a leader within my community. I am continuing to help grow the computer science program in my school by encouraging other students to learn about programming, and promoting STEM to younger grades by showing them projects I have worked on. My favorite project that I am currently working on is a program that helps students located in Sub-Saharan Africa access STEM related education. I initiated and currently manage a robotics program at St. Thomas Amagaro Girls Secondary School in Amagaro, Kenya. It’s so rewarding to share my love of programming with those who wouldn’t otherwise have access to it. When I'm not coding, I enjoy playing the guitar, tennis, skiing, and working my part-time job at Starbucks. Fun fact: I programmed this website by teaching myself HTML and CSS!

Computer Science
I strive to be a leader within my community, and because of that, I took on many leadership roles within my school this year. Robotics
STAGGS Robotics Team in Amagaro, Kenya
In October 2022, I attended a meeting with members of Kiwimbi Global, an organization dedicated to providing education to students located in Sub-Saharan Africa. As Project Manager of the robotics team, SO BOTZ, I worked amongst a small group to connect our team, Kiwimbi Global, and St. Thomas Amagaro Girls Secondary School. I initiated and currently manage a robotics team at the school. To begin this collaboration, my team donated Scratch workbooks and VEX robots to their local community center and library. At St. Thomas, we helped the teachers develop their own robotics club. To assist them through the process, I continuously update a website with tutorial videos made by members of the team and myself. I stay in contact with the teachers through whatsapp at the school and pay attention to a question box on the website, where students can ask questions or request specific tutorials to be created. On the website, I created videos teaching them how to create a google drive account and a scratch account. With these tutorials, they were able to send their first emails to my account, where they discussed how thrilled they were for the program and the progress they had made at that point. I run a meeting with them virtually every other Saturday to congratulate them on their progress, help them however is necessary, and continue building a strong relationship with the students. At the first meeting, I created a game to play with the students to help them practice critical thinking skills that are a necessity in computer science. The students seemed to be reluctant to join in, and weren’t used to an environment where they could speak up. However, these students have come a long way. At meetings, they are eager to learn, speak, and are endlessly appreciative of the robotics program we helped create. It is so rewarding to see how far they have come, and I have faith this team will continue for many years to come. They began attending regional science fair competitions in March of 2023, where they showcase their robot and give a presentation about what they have done on the team. At their first local competition, they won first place and were ecstatic with their progress. Shortly after on March 12th, I was invited to speak as a representative of my team virtually at the 67th NGO CSW Forum, which was a virtual event that focused on the empowerment of women and hosted by one of the main UN organs within the United Nations, the UNCSW. I was able to talk about my experience working with the students in Amagaro and how it impacted my life as well as their lives. Our Forum focused on women helping women, and spreading opportunities across the world. They continued this success and won their regional, county, and national competition.
Project Management
In my junior year, I was selected to be the project manager of the robotics team. In this position, I worked to turn my team into a FIRST Impact team that strives to be an environment of learning, camaraderie, and teamwork. I work hard to make my team a place where all members feel comfortable asking questions and have a passion for what they are doing. It is important that members are offered new opportunities, try new things, and are all given tasks they are eager to complete and have an interest in. I have meetings with the software, build, design, and finance/marketing leads every Monday to keep the team organized and on the same track. I plan to continue being a project manager until I graduate, and mentor FIRST teams in the future.
Software
My robotics team programs in java using the WPI libraries provided by FIRST. During the Rapid React competition in 2022 I began researching and programming color detection for red and blue colored balls using a REV robotics color sensor. Over the summer going into the 2023 season, I created a basic drivetrain tutorial that walks new members through the process of programming using the WPI libraries, VS Code, and Github. It teaches them step by step how to run motors in a program, use joystick inputs, and implement subsystems into commands. In the 2023 season, Charged Up, I worked on a vision program that uses a limelight. It has the capability to detect reflective tape and april tags using different pipelines. The program allows the robot to autonomously use commands to align with targets, drive to a specific distance away from the target, and detect alignment.
Drive Team
I began my experience with the drive team as the second technician throughout the 2021 preseason and the beginning of the 2022 season. At the final competition in 2022, I was the first technician and my team became an alliance captain for the first time. During this competition, I went through training to become the coach of the drive team. During the 2022 preseason, our team attended two competitions where I was able to put this training into action, and was able to coach the drive team to get first place in the Raritan Valley Robot Rumble. Throughout the 2023 season, I will coach the drive team at three regional competitions and train younger members to take over my position when I graduate.
